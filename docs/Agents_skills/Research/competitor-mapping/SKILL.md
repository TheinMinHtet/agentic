## Overview
This skill helps in systematically identifying and analyzing competitors within a given market. It provides a structured approach to understand the competitive landscape, including direct and indirect competitors, their offerings, market share, strategies, and key differentiators.

## Usage
To use this skill, provide a description of the product, service, or market you want to analyze. The skill will help outline the process of competitor identification and analysis.

## Steps
1.  **Define Market/Offering**: Clearly define the market segment or the product/service for which competitor analysis is needed.
2.  **Identify Competitors**: List direct and indirect competitors.
3.  **Gather Data**: Collect information on competitors' products, pricing, marketing, strengths, weaknesses, and market share.
4.  **Map Positioning**: Visualize competitor positioning based on key attributes (e.g., price vs. quality).
5.  **Output**: A structured overview of the competitive landscape.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Analyze competitors for a new AI-powered personal finance app targeting millennials."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **Direct Competitors**: Mint, Personal Capital, YNAB, Rocket Money
-   **Indirect Competitors**: Traditional banks' budgeting tools, Excel spreadsheets
-   **Key Attributes to Compare**: AI features, budgeting tools, investment tracking, user interface, pricing, customer support
-   **Strengths (e.g., Mint)**: Established user base, comprehensive features
-   **Weaknesses (e.g., Mint)**: Data privacy concerns, less advanced AI
-   **Positioning Map**: (Description of a conceptual 2x2 matrix: e.g., "High-tech / Low-cost" vs. "Traditional / High-cost")
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Outdated Data**: Market landscapes change rapidly; ensure data sources are current.
*   **Bias**: Avoid personal biases when evaluating competitors.
*   **Over-focus on Direct Competitors**: Remember to include indirect and emerging threats.
*   **Information Overload**: Focus on key, actionable insights rather than exhaustive data collection.
