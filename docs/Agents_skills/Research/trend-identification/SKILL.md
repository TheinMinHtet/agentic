## Overview
This skill provides a structured approach to identifying, analyzing, and understanding market trends. It focuses on recognizing patterns, shifts, and emerging forces that can influence a business or market, helping to uncover opportunities and anticipate threats.

## Usage
Provide a market or industry description to initiate trend identification. The skill will help outline the process of researching and synthesizing trend information.

## Steps
1.  **Define Scope**: Specify the industry, market, or area of interest for trend analysis.
2.  **Data Collection**: Gather information from various sources (e.g., industry reports, news, academic papers, social media, expert opinions).
3.  **Pattern Recognition**: Identify recurring themes, shifts in consumer behavior, technological advancements, regulatory changes, and economic indicators.
4.  **Impact Analysis**: Assess the potential impact of identified trends on the target market/business (opportunities, threats, strategic implications).
5.  **Output**: A concise summary of key trends and their implications.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Identify key trends in the electric vehicle market for the next 5 years."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **Electrification Acceleration**: Rapid increase in EV adoption, policy support.
-   **Battery Technology Advancements**: Improved range, faster charging, lower costs.
-   **Charging Infrastructure Expansion**: Growth in public and private charging networks.
-   **Autonomous Driving Integration**: EVs as a platform for self-driving features.
-   **Subscription Models**: Emergence of EV subscription services.
-   **Impact**: Increased competition, demand for sustainable supply chains, new business models.
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Signal vs. Noise**: Distinguishing genuine trends from fads or short-term fluctuations.
*   **Confirmation Bias**: Only seeking information that supports existing beliefs about trends.
*   **Over-reliance on Single Source**: Diversify data sources to get a holistic view.
*   **Lack of Actionability**: Trends are only useful if they lead to actionable insights or strategic adjustments.
