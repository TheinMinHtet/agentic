## Overview
This skill provides a structured methodology for calculating market size using the TAM, SAM, and SOM framework. It helps in understanding the total potential market, the portion accessible by a specific business model, and the realistic share that can be captured.

## Usage
Provide details about your product/service and its target market. The skill will outline the process for estimating TAM, SAM, and SOM.

## Steps
1.  **Define TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: Estimate the total revenue opportunity if 100% market share is achieved for a product or service.
2.  **Define SAM (Serviceable Available Market)**: Identify the segment of the TAM that can be reached effectively with the current business model and capabilities.
3.  **Define SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: Determine the realistic portion of the SAM that a company can capture, considering competition, resources, and strategic focus.
4.  **Data Collection**: Gather relevant demographic, economic, and industry data for calculations.
5.  **Calculation & Refinement**: Apply appropriate methodologies (top-down, bottom-up) and refine estimates.
6.  **Output**: Clear figures for TAM, SAM, and SOM with underlying assumptions.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Estimate market size for a new B2B SaaS platform offering AI-driven customer support solutions for small to medium businesses (SMBs) in North America."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **TAM (AI-driven customer support market globally)**: $50 Billion
-   **SAM (North American SMBs needing AI-driven customer support)**: $15 Billion
-   **SOM (Realistic share achievable in 5 years)**: $1.5 Billion (10% of SAM)
-   **Assumptions**: (e.g., Average contract value, number of SMBs in NA, adoption rates, competitive landscape)
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Over-optimistic Estimates**: Inflating numbers can lead to poor strategic decisions.
*   **Lack of Data**: Insufficient or unreliable data can severely impact accuracy.
*   **Confusing TAM/SAM/SOM**: Misunderstanding the distinctions can lead to misallocated resources.
*   **Static Analysis**: Market sizes are dynamic; regular re-evaluation is necessary.
*   **Ignoring Niche Markets**: Sometimes a smaller SOM with high capture is more valuable than a huge, unattainable TAM.
