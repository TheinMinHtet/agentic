## Overview
This skill provides a structured approach to generating and evaluating brand names. It covers ideation techniques, linguistic considerations, market testing, and crucial legal checks to ensure a strong, memorable, and protectable brand identity.

## Usage
Provide context about the product, service, or company that needs a name, including its target audience and unique selling points. The skill outlines the process for developing and selecting brand names.

## Steps
1.  **Define Naming Objectives**: Clarify what the name should convey (e.g., innovation, reliability, fun).
2.  **Brainstorming & Ideation**: Generate a wide range of names using various strategies (e.g., descriptive, evocative, invented, acronyms, founder names).
3.  **Linguistic & Cultural Review**: Check for pronunciation difficulties, negative connotations, or unintended meanings in relevant languages and cultures.
4.  **Market & Audience Testing**: Gather feedback from target audiences on memorability, appeal, and relevance.
5.  **Availability Check**: Verify domain names, social media handles, and relevant trademarks.
6.  **Legal Review**: Consult with legal experts for full trademark search and registration potential.
7.  **Selection**: Choose the optimal name based on all analyses.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Develop naming strategies for a new eco-friendly cleaning product targeting environmentally conscious families."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **Strategy**: Focus on evocative names that suggest nature, purity, and effectiveness.
-   **Ideation Examples**: GreenClean, EcoShine, PureBloom, TerraWash, Solstice Clean
-   **Considerations**: Avoid names that sound clinical or use harsh chemical terms. Ensure global appeal for future expansion.
-   **Initial Checks**: Verify domain availability for top choices.
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Rushing Legal Checks**: Not performing thorough trademark searches can lead to costly rebranding or legal issues.
*   **Ignoring Target Audience**: Choosing a name that appeals internally but not to the desired customer base.
*   **Overly Complex or Hard-to-Pronounce Names**: Hinders memorability and word-of-mouth.
*   **Lack of Differentiation**: Choosing a name that is too generic or similar to competitors.
*   **Future-Proofing**: Selecting a name that is too narrow and might not scale with future product line expansion.
