## Overview
This skill provides a structured framework for defining a brand's unique tone of voice (ToV). A consistent ToV ensures the brand sounds like itself in every interaction, building trust and strengthening its identity.

## Usage
Provide information about the brand identity, target audience, and desired emotional response to initiate tone configuration.

## Steps
1.  **Analyze Brand Identity**: Define the core values and personality traits that the brand embodies.
2.  **Define Tone Attributes**: Create a set of "We are X, but not Y" attributes (e.g., "We are professional, but not stuffy; we are playful, but not reckless").
3.  **Identify Audience Contexts**: Determine how the tone should shift depending on the channel or situation (e.g., social media vs. formal reports vs. customer support).
4.  **Create Writing Guidelines**: Establish specific rules for word choice, sentence structure, punctuation, and use of humor/emojis.
5.  **Develop Examples**: Provide "Before/After" examples of how to rewrite content to fit the brand's tone.
6.  **Output**: A cohesive Tone of Voice style guide.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Define ToV for a friendly, approachable bank targeting young professionals."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **Core Traits**: Empathetic, transparent, uncomplicated.
-   **"We are X, not Y"**: 
    - We are human, not robotic.
    - We are helpful, not pushy.
    - We are clear, not jargon-heavy.
-   **Writing Guidelines**: Use active voice, keep sentences short, use 'you' and 'we' to create connection. Avoid acronyms unless defined.
-   **Example Rewrite**: 
    - Standard: "Your account balance has been debited for the monthly service fee."
    - ToV Optimized: "We've just processed your monthly account fee. You can find the details in your dashboard."
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Too Broad**: Tone definitions that aren't specific enough to guide actual writing.
*   **Inconsistency**: Failing to apply the tone consistently across different teams (marketing, support, sales).
*   **Assuming One Size Fits All**: Not adjusting the tone slightly for the channel (e.g., Twitter vs. whitepaper).
*   **Tone Evolution**: Forgetting to update the tone as the brand matures or the audience shifts.
*   **Ignoring Feedback**: Not analyzing how the audience responds to the voice and failing to refine accordingly.
