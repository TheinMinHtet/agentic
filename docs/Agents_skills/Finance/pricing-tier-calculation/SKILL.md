## Overview
This skill provides a structured approach to developing effective pricing tiers and strategies. It considers various factors such as perceived value, operational costs, competitive landscape, and customer segmentation to determine optimal price points and offerings for different tiers.

## Usage
Provide details about your product/service, its features, target customer segments, and relevant cost information. The skill outlines the process for defining and calculating pricing tiers.

## Steps
1.  **Define Value**: Identify the perceived value of your product/service for different customer segments.
2.  **Analyze Costs**: Determine fixed and variable costs associated with delivering each tier or feature set.
3.  **Competitive Analysis**: Research competitor pricing, models, and perceived value.
4.  **Set Pricing Objectives**: Define goals (e.g., market penetration, profit maximization, revenue growth).
5.  **Develop Tier Structures**: Design different pricing tiers (e.g., Basic, Pro, Enterprise) with distinct features and benefits.
6.  **Calculate Price Points**: Determine the optimal price for each tier, considering cost-plus, value-based, or competitive pricing methods.
7.  **Test & Iterate**: Validate pricing with target customers and be prepared to adjust.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Product: SaaS project management tool. Features: basic (task management), pro (collaboration, integrations), enterprise (advanced reporting, dedicated support). Target: SMBs, large enterprises. Goal: Maximize revenue."

Output (Conceptual):
-   **Basic Tier**: $19/user/month (focused on individual users/small teams)
-   **Pro Tier**: $49/user/month (value for teams needing collaboration and advanced features)
-   **Enterprise Tier**: Custom pricing (based on specific needs for large organizations)
-   **Rationale**: Balances feature differentiation, cost recovery, and competitive positioning.
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Underpricing**: Leaving money on the table or devaluing the product.
*   **Overpricing**: Alienating potential customers or losing to competitors.
*   **Overly Complex Tiers**: Confusing customers and hindering adoption.
*   **Ignoring Customer Willingness to Pay**: Not aligning prices with what customers perceive as fair value.
*   **Static Pricing**: Not adapting pricing as market conditions or product features evolve.
