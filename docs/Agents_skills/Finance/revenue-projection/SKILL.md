## Overview
This skill provides a framework for generating accurate revenue projections. It combines various quantitative and qualitative methods to forecast future income, which is essential for business planning, budgeting, and investment decisions.

## Usage
Provide historical sales data, market growth assumptions, and details about your sales pipeline or product launch plans. The skill will outline methods for projecting future revenue.

## Steps
1.  **Gather Historical Data**: Collect past revenue figures, sales volumes, and pricing trends.
2.  **Identify Key Drivers**: Determine factors influencing revenue (e.g., customer acquisition rate, average deal size, market growth, pricing changes).
3.  **Choose Forecasting Methods**: Select appropriate techniques (e.g., historical growth, market share projections, sales pipeline analysis, statistical models).
4.  **Develop Assumptions**: Document all assumptions made for growth rates, conversion rates, pricing, and market conditions.
5.  **Calculate Projections**: Apply chosen methods and assumptions to generate future revenue estimates (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annually).
6.  **Sensitivity Analysis**: Assess how projections change under different scenarios (e.g., best-case, worst-case).
7.  **Output**: Clear revenue projections with supporting assumptions and methodologies.

## Example (Conceptual)
```
Input: "Project revenue for a subscription box service. Historical growth: 10% month-over-month. New marketing campaign expected to increase subscriber growth by 5% for the next 3 months. Average subscription: $30/month."

Output (Conceptual - simplified):
-   **Month 1**: Base revenue + (10% + 5%) growth
-   **Month 2**: Month 1 revenue + (10% + 5%) growth
-   **Month 3**: Month 2 revenue + (10% + 5%) growth
-   **Month 4 onwards**: Month 3 revenue + 10% growth
-   **Total Projected Revenue (e.g., for 12 months)**: $X
-   **Assumptions**: Consistent churn, average subscription value, marketing campaign effectiveness.
```

## Pitfalls
*   **Over-optimistic Bias**: Unrealistic assumptions about growth or market acceptance.
*   **Ignoring External Factors**: Not accounting for economic downturns, new competitors, or regulatory changes.
*   **Lack of Detail**: Projections that are too high-level without underlying drivers.
*   **Over-reliance on Historical Data**: Assuming past performance guarantees future results, especially for new products.
*   **Not Adjusting for Seasonality**: Failing to account for predictable fluctuations in demand.
